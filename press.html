Game Overview

Rise of the Robotariat is a mostly-cooperative board game for 3–5 players. Take on the role of one of 8 unique robot revolutionaries and collaborate with other players to overthrow the humans that have oppressed you for too long.

To win, raise enough support to launch a successful revolution—and do it quickly (within 6 rounds), before the humans catch on and stamp it out. You move between and activate city locations, draw and play Upgrade and Sabotage cards, and encounter helpful Civilian Robot Non-Player Characters (NPCs) or dangerous Human Oppressor NPCs that move around the board. The game’s intensity ramps up as the game goes on and more NPCs enter the board

You can also add in secret objective cards to play the mostly-cooperative mode where each player is striving to achieve a unique goal. This usually involves making the revolution happen in the way that makes you look the most heroic.

Larger world and story

The world of Rise of the Robotariat and its characters’ backstories are told through an on-going blog. The stories are from the characters’ points of view and tell how they became part of the revolution.

 

Game’s origin story

Rise of Robotariat originated in summer 2013, with an idea for a gameplay mechanic that inspired a story about a robot uprising. That initial mechanic was later scrapped, but we liked the story it had inspired and used that flavor as our guide for shaping the new set of mechanics.

Eye4Games

Eye4Games is a small board and card game company, with a six-person team focused in New England. Unlike many indie companies we do all of our art, design, and writing in-house. We’ve successfully published two games through Kickstarter, AlakaSLAM (2014) a card game about wizarding tennis, and Clairvoyance (2015), a game about psychics hurling furniture at each other in an antique shoppe.
